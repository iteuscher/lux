About

 

What is Lux?

Lux is a web-based activity to help a community become more aware of its silent identites

 

How does it work?

A Lux is created with questions like: ‘Have you been bullied at school?’ or ‘Have you been sexually harassed by someone from our community?’ A link to the Lux is shared out to the members who each privatly answer the questions. All answers are stored anonymously. Then at an assembly or meeting, each member heads to the shared link and is anonymously given the answers of someone else. The questions are read aloud and people stand up when their given answer is yes. Although numbers and graphs can make the same points, this exercise is especially powerful because you see your peers standing and know that they are giving voice to the identity of someone else in the room.

 

Inspiration for Lux

The idea and structure of Lux is based off similar activites led by affinity groups at Head-Royce School in Oakland, CA. The inspiration to create a website to run Lux activities came from the #MeToo movement and an increased interest to understand the pevelence of issues like sexual harrasment that are too often ignored.

 

Meet the Creator

Lux is designed and created by Isaac Teuscher. Lux began as a 2017 Winter semester final project in Mr. Brain Sea's Adv. Topics of Computer Science class at Head-Royce School. You can contact Isaac with any questions and connect with him on social media below.
